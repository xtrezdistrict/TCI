Who are we?

Trauma and Counselling Interventions is made of a group of therapists whose target clients are families, learners, men, women, youths, adolescents, and special populations. All our therapists are registered with the Allied Health Profession Council of Zimbabwe (AHPCZ). We have social workers on the team too. The grouping was formed in 2018, with the focus of targeting vulnerable populations and persons who cannot afford mental health and those who try to solve mental health issues metaphysically by coining them with evil spirits. With an increase in drug and person abuse, we, TCI have scaled up our operations to other districts and provinces from Harare Metropolitan province.

Our operational objectives

  1. to increase mental health services and interventions to marginalized communities
  2. to advocate for increased access to mental health services to school-going learners, school leavers, and couples hence reducing drug abuse and suicides.
  3. To provide mental rehabilitation to victims of abuse and drug abusers.
  4. To source relevant therapy drugs such as tobacco patches and aversion therapy drugs.
  5. To establish whistle-blowers on drug peddlers.
  6. To establish anti-drugs, peer educators.
  7. To establish problem offloading portals and platforms.
  8. To give reproductive health education.
  9. To provide career guidance and build a positive self-concept.
  10. To provide menopause counseling.
  11. To provide counseling to those with disabilities and their families.

Regulatory body: - Allied Health Professions Council of Zimbabwe

Consenting– the client has a right to consent to any form of intervention that the Centre may want to engage in the therapeutic process.

Confidentiality– the client has the right to confidentiality to any information that concerns him or her. In an event that legal institutions may request certain information; the terms of confidentiality must be explained in that context therein. In the case of a minor guardian will assume the terms of confidentiality

Mental Health and Medical guidelines– the Centre will adhere to medical guidelines in protecting the welfare of the client.

Dual Relationship– to reduces bias and exploitation in whatever manner the Centre will not engage any therapist who has another relationship with the client.

Dual counseling-all– protocols will be observed if any need arises for dual counseling.

Covid-19 protocols– social distancing, physical distancing, masking up, and providing vaccinated therapists
